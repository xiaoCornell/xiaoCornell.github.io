Xiao Wang (王骁)

I am a postdoctoral associate in the Department of Physics at Cornell University, collaborating with Prof. Eun-Ah Kim. I obtained my Ph.D. from the Tsung-Dao Lee Institute at Shanghai Jiao Tong University as a first-batch T. D. Lee doctor.

My research focuses on integrable systems and conformal field theory, together with applications in condensed matter physics, nonequilibrium physics, quantum simulation, and quantum information.

Research Areas

Questions that motivate my work

Integrable Systems

Exact and near-exact structures in quantum many-body systems, with an emphasis on field-theoretic and lattice-model formulations.

Conformal Field Theory

Universal critical behavior, spectral structures, and analytical tools that connect statistical mechanics, field theory, and experiment.

Nonequilibrium Dynamics

Time-dependent quantum dynamics, driven spin chains, and real-time evolution far from equilibrium.

Quantum Matter Applications

Applications to condensed matter physics, quantum simulation platforms, and quantum information-inspired problems.
